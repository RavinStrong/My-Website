What is Horror?

Horror is a genre of storytelling that is meant to shock, scare and thrill its audience. Horror as its own genre is quite broad. Here I will breakdown the main sub-genres that make up horror

Slasher

A slasher is a genre of horror almost exclusively found in film where a killer stalks a group of people and murders them, usually with sharp tools. This specific subgenre has many tropes that may exist in other subgenres but are typical of slashers. The villains of a slasher are almost always human; commonly , they were also once victims of some past violence. This is the “explanation” of why the killer acts as they do. Another genre trope is the “final girl”; the final girl is the last woman standing who typically defeats or unmasks the killer. While final girls can be found throughout horror, they are most famously a part of slashers. While slashers do tend to have violent scenes, they usually are not very gory; however, that can change depending on the film.

Psychological

Psychological horror is a subgenre that explores a character's mental and psychological states. Psychological horror can also be referred to as a thriller; it aims to create suspense throughout the story, resulting in a climax. A common trope is that these stories seek to disturb their viewers by focusing on a character who loses grasp on reality, revealing them as outsiders, leaving the narrator unsettled, and questioning everything they know about the story. Psychological horror is rarely gory; if there is any gore, it is generally during the climactic ending. Psychological horror is often paired with other genres like supernatural, creating its own unique subgenre with its own tropes

Supernatural

Supernatural horror it's pretty self-explanatory; it's a subgenre that revolves around supernatural occurrences. This includes anything to do with ghosts, the afterlife, devils or demons. This subgenre has been around for centuries. There are tales dating back hundreds of years that are about the supernatural. Supernatural horror is often paired with other sub genres like thriller or body horror..

Body Horror

Body horror or biological horror is a subgenre that is focused on the grotesque and gore. Gore refers to violence or bloodshed, while gore is often found in body horror. It is not always a requirement. Body horror also includes mutated, deformed, or manipulated human bodies or other creatures. The grotesque nature of these films can make the audience feel disturbed and sometimes disgusted, though the audience's reaction is based on the level of body horror in the story. Many believe body horror is a genre that is "gore for the sake of gore." It is often just a representation of the commentary an author or director is trying to make. Commonly, changing a character's physical body is often a representation of something internally going on with a character, like moral corruption or something similar. These physical changes are also often a representation of a commentary on class, race, or society.
