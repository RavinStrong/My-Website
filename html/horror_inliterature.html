The Fall of The House of The Usher by Edgar Allen Poe

Edgar Allen Poe is one of the first writers in America to popularize the scary, dark, and macabre. He is famous for many of his works, like his poem "The Raven," but one of his most influential works is The Fall of The House of The Usher. This short gothic horror story is significant because it explores the supernatural and psychological. The story is about the unnamed narrator arriving at his friend Rodrick Usher's home. Once he arrives, he sees the home is in disarray and seemingly falling apart. The house represents the Usher family as the Usher family is an aristocratic family with a "Pure" lineage; however, that cannot save them from their grizzly end. This short story has inspired many other horror works throughout history (and is my favorite:)

The Shining by Steven King

The Shining by Steven King is a famous American horror novel. We follow a man named Jack and his family as he becomes a caretaker of a hotel and strange things begin happening, sending Jack into a dangerous downward spiral. This novel is very influential to modern horror, its film of the same name is considered a work of genius. Both the book and film have greatly inspired and changed the genre of horror.
