The Epic of Gilgamesh

In ancient Mesopotamia, it was believed that all illness and misfortune was caused by hauntings, creating a slew of stories that lend themselves to the horror genre. In the famous story, a king named Gilgamesh is challenged by a warrior. Once Gilgamesh defeats this warrior, Enkidu, They travel around completing various tasks, such as slaying the guardian of the legendary Cedar Forest. They even kill the “Bull of Heaven.” This insults one of their goddesses, and she sentences Enkidu to death. This epic tale of monsters, gods, and even murderous humans is considered one of the oldest horror stories. 

The Horrors of Ancient Greece

Many of us are familiar with ancient Greek stories, whether about their many gods, monsters, or demigods. But many of these stories are considered the great-great-great grandfathers of horror. The story of Oedipus is a great example. Oedipus is determined to find a kingslayer, unaware throughout his journey that he IS the kingslayer; he even marries the windowed queen without realizing she is his birth mother, which sounds like a psychological horror to me. 

An Ancient Egyptian Ghost Story

“Ghost Story” is an ancient Egyptian tale dating back to 1880 CE, while some scholars disagree on some details of the story. The main plot is about a high priest who spends a night in the necropolis, where he encounters a spirit who is restless because his tomb is falling apart. After that night, the priest gathers a group to find where he is buried in hopes of restoring his tomb. Unfortunately, the rest of the story is lost, so we do not know how it ends. However, this tale of a restless spirit is nothing short of ghostly horror.
